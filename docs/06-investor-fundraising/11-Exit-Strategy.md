## 1. Exit Philosophy

Sovereign AIOps is built to be an enduring, independent company. We are not optimizing for a quick acquisition. However, responsible governance requires a clear understanding of exit options that maximize shareholder value. Our strategy prioritizes building a category-leading business that creates multiple high-value exit paths, giving investors optionality rather than dependency on a single outcome.

**Primary Path:** Scale to $500M+ ARR and pursue an IPO on NASDAQ
**Secondary Path:** Strategic acquisition at $2B+ enterprise value
**Timeline:** 5-8 years from Series A (2031-2034)

## 4. Dual-Track Process

For maximum optionality, we plan a dual-track process beginning in 2030-2031:

### Phase 1: Preparation (12-18 months before event)
- Engage investment bank (Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, or Qatalyst Partners for tech M&A)
- Audit financials (Big 4 auditor engaged by Series B)
- Clean up cap table, contracts, and IP assignments
- Implement public-company-grade financial reporting (GAAP)
- Build independent board with public company experience

### Phase 2: Execution (6-12 months)
- File S-1 confidentially while simultaneously engaging 3-5 strategic acquirers
- Evaluate offers against IPO pricing range
- Choose path that maximizes long-term shareholder value
- If acquisition offers exceed IPO implied valuation by >30%, consider sale
- If IPO market is strong and company is growing >40%, proceed with IPO

### Phase 3: Post-Transaction
- IPO: Lock-up period (180 days), followed by orderly secondary market for early investors
- Acquisition: Negotiate for upfront cash consideration (minimize earnouts); founder retention incentives for 2-year transition

## 6. Investor Liquidity Considerations

### 6.1 Secondary Market Opportunities

- At Series B or later rounds, facilitate secondary sales for seed and angel investors seeking partial liquidity
- Target 10-20% of existing investor shares eligible for secondary
- Use secondary as a valuation benchmark for tax and accounting purposes

### 6.2 Timeline to Liquidity

| Scenario | Path | Timeline | Expected Multiple |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fast Exit | Strategic acquisition | 3-4 years (2029-2030) | 5-12x |
| Moderate | Late-stage acquisition or IPO | 5-6 years (2031-2032) | 12-25x |
| Long-Term | IPO at scale | 6-8 years (2032-2034) | 20-72x |

### 6.3 Downside Protection

- Liquidation preference: 1x non-participating preferred (standard Series A terms)
- Anti-dilution: Weighted average broad-based
- Board representation: Series A lead receives 1 board seat
- Information rights: Monthly financials, quarterly board deck, annual audit
- Pro-rata rights: Series A investors have pro-rata participation in future rounds

## 7. Key Takeaway

Sovereign AIOps offers investors exposure to a high-growth enterprise software company in a $18B market with multiple value-creation pathways. The combination of autonomous remediation technology, zero churn, and ERP-native integration creates a defensible position that is attractive to both public market investors (IPO) and strategic acquirers (M&A). The dual-track approach ensures maximum optionality and valuation optimization regardless of market conditions.
